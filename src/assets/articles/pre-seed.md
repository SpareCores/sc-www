This follows the [NGI Search grant](/article/ngi-search-grant) we received in 2024, which
gave us the runway to build the core of the Spare Cores ecosystem.
This pre-seed round gives us the resources to grow the team and move faster on the product side.

## What We Are Working On

First of all, we are using the funds to hire developers. After the NGI Search grant
period ended, the project ran as a single-person effort for almost a year, which was
fine for keeping things alive but not for moving fast. I am extremely happy to say
that we have already reached 4 FTE.

We remain committed to our open-source work. The transparency we bring to the cloud server
landscape -- hardware specs, benchmark data, pricing -- stays open and freely accessible.
That is not changing.

On top of that data, we are building a SaaS product aimed at data scientists, ML engineers,
and AI practitioners who run batch jobs with varying resource requirements. The idea is
simple: track what your job actually uses, and find the cloud server that fits it well
without over-provisioning. We have been laying the groundwork for this with the
`resource-tracker` open-source library and our Metaflow integration, described in the
[Optimizing Resource Usage of Batch Jobs](/article/metaflow-resource-tracker) article.

Exciting times ahead! 🚀
